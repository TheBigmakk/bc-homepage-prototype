Inspection work doesn’t break on site. It breaks at the desk.

Inspectors don’t struggle to collect information. They struggle to reconstruct it later.

Every building is different, but most inspection software forces rigid forms and field-by-field input. On site, that slows judgement. So people do what works: they take photos. Hundreds of them. Photography is fast, natural, and captures context better than notes ever will.

The cost shows up later. Sorting images. Renaming files. Dragging photos into reports. Rebuilding context that was obvious on site, but is now fragile or gone.

Beyond Condition starts with images, not forms.

You take photos as normal, on any device. Beyond Condition ingests them and extracts the metadata already embedded inside each image — location, time, order, and authorship.

That information becomes the structure. Images are organised automatically. Fields populate themselves. Reports, schedules, and map-based outputs are generated without manual drag-and-drop work.

We don’t optimise for less time on site. We remove the administrative drag that punishes professional judgement.
